SECTION 2 — DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

2.1 HOSPITALISATION
Admission to a Hospital for a minimum period of 24 consecutive hours for
medically necessary treatment of an illness or injury.

2.2 PRE-AUTHORISATION (PRE-AUTH)
A written approval obtained from the Insurance Company or its designated
Third Party Administrator (TPA) prior to a planned or emergency
hospitalisation for cashless claim processing.

2.3 WAITING PERIOD
The time period from the Policy Start Date during which certain benefits
under this Policy are not available to the Insured Person. Any claim
arising from a condition during the applicable waiting period shall be
rejected and no benefit shall be payable.

2.4 MEDICALLY NECESSARY TREATMENT
Any treatment, test, medication, or hospital stay that is:
(a) Required for the diagnosis or treatment of the illness or injury
(b) Consistent with the clinical protocols and standard medical guidelines
(c) Not performed for experimental, cosmetic, or preventive purposes
(d) Not in excess of the dosage, frequency, or duration prescribed by
    recognised clinical standards

2.5 INSURED PERSON
The individual named in the Policy Schedule who is covered under this Policy.

2.6 NETWORK HOSPITAL
A hospital empanelled with HDFC ERGO or its TPA for cashless treatment.
City Care Hospital, Mumbai is a registered Network Hospital under this Policy.

2.7 FRAUD
Any deliberate misrepresentation, concealment of facts, falsification of
documents, or overbilling by a hospital, provider, or insured person with
the intent to obtain a benefit not legitimately owed under this Policy.

SECTION 3 — WAITING PERIOD CLAUSES
================================================================================

3.1 INITIAL WAITING PERIOD — ALL ILLNESSES
A waiting period of 30 (thirty) days applies from the Policy Start Date
for all illnesses except accidental injuries. No claim for hospitalisation
arising from any illness shall be admissible if the date of admission falls
within 30 days of the Policy Start Date.

3.2 GENERAL HOSPITALISATION WAITING PERIOD
*** CLAUSE 3.2 — PRIMARY FRAUD DETECTION TRIGGER FOR CASE 1 ***

A waiting period of 24 (twenty-four) calendar months applies from the
Policy Start Date for all non-accidental general hospitalisation claims.

This means:
- The Insured Person must have held an active, continuously renewed policy
  for a minimum of 24 complete calendar months before a general
  hospitalisation claim becomes admissible.
- The waiting period is counted from the original Policy Start Date and
  is NOT reset upon renewal, provided the policy is renewed without a break.
- If the policy lapses and is re-issued, the waiting period restarts from
  the new Policy Start Date.

Conditions covered under general hospitalisation waiting period include but
are not limited to: fever of unknown origin, acute febrile illness, viral
infections, general observation admissions, and non-specific medical
conditions not listed under critical illness or accident categories.

EXAMPLE FOR CLARITY:
If a policy is issued on 25 January 2025, the general hospitalisation
waiting period expires on 24 January 2027. Any general hospitalisation
claim submitted between 25 January 2025 and 24 January 2027 shall be
automatically rejected under this clause.

3.3 SPECIFIC DISEASE WAITING PERIOD
A waiting period of 24 (twenty-four) calendar months applies for the
following listed conditions:
- Cataract
- Hernia (all types)
- Joint replacement surgery
- Sinusitis and related disorders
- Tonsillitis and adenoids
- Varicose veins
- Calculi (kidney, bile duct, urinary)
- Piles, fissures, fistula

3.4 PRE-EXISTING DISEASE (PED) WAITING PERIOD
A waiting period of 36 (thirty-six) calendar months applies for any
Pre-Existing Disease declared or discovered at the time of policy purchase.
After the completion of 36 months of continuous coverage, Pre-Existing
Diseases shall be covered subject to all other policy terms.

3.5 WAITING PERIOD — CRITICAL ILLNESS EXCEPTION
The waiting period clauses under 3.1, 3.2, and 3.3 DO NOT apply to:
- Accidental injuries requiring emergency hospitalisation
- Heart attack (Myocardial Infarction)
- Stroke
- Cancer (first diagnosis)
- Road traffic accidents

Note: Dengue Fever is NOT classified as a critical illness under this
Policy and is subject to the applicable waiting period clauses unless
the Insured Person has completed the required waiting period.

SECTION 4 — DENGUE FEVER COVERAGE
================================================================================

4.1 DENGUE COVERAGE — GENERAL TERMS
Dengue Fever (ICD-10: A97) is covered under this Policy subject to:
(a) The Insured Person having completed the applicable waiting period
    under Clause 3.2 (24 months general hospitalisation waiting period)
(b) Positive diagnostic confirmation via NS1 Antigen test or
    IgM/IgG serology test from an NAABL or NABL accredited laboratory
(c) Hospitalisation being medically necessary as determined by a
    registered medical practitioner
(d) All treatment administered being consistent with recognised clinical
    protocols for dengue management

4.2 COVERED EXPENSES — DENGUE
The following expenses are covered for a confirmed dengue hospitalisation:
- Room rent (as per plan sub-limits)
- ICU charges if required
- Consultation and specialist fees
- Diagnostic tests: CBC, NS1, dengue serology, liver function tests
- IV fluids and electrolyte management
- Medications as prescribed by treating physician
- Platelet-supportive therapy as per clinical protocol
- Nursing and attendant charges as per hospital policy

4.3 SUB-LIMITS — DENGUE HOSPITALISATION
Room Rent                  : Up to INR 3,000 per day (General Ward)
ICU Charges                : Up to INR 7,500 per day
Diagnostic Tests           : Covered up to actuals within Sum Insured
Doctor Consultation        : Up to INR 750 per visit, max 2 visits per day

SECTION 5 — CLINICAL PROTOCOL AND MEDICATION FREQUENCY GUIDELINES
================================================================================

5.1 POLICY ON MEDICALLY NECESSARY DOSAGE
*** CLAUSE 5.1 — PRIMARY FRAUD DETECTION TRIGGER FOR CASE 2 ***

This Policy covers only those medications and injectable treatments that
are administered in accordance with:
(a) The prescribing physician's documented instructions
(b) Recognised clinical treatment protocols published by the Indian
    Council of Medical Research (ICMR), World Health Organization (WHO),
    or equivalent recognised medical authority
(c) The manufacturer's approved prescribing information

Any medication or injectable billed in excess of the clinically approved
frequency, dosage, or duration shall be treated as non-medically necessary
and will not be covered under this Policy.

5.2 INJECTABLE MEDICATIONS — DENGUE MANAGEMENT PROTOCOL
*** CLAUSE 5.2 — SPECIFIC FRAUD DETECTION TRIGGER FOR CASE 2 ***

As per the ICMR National Guidelines for Clinical Management of Dengue
(2023 Edition) and the WHO Dengue Treatment Protocol (Revised 2022),
the following restrictions apply to platelet-supportive injectable
therapy used in dengue management:

PLATELET-SUPPORTIVE / HAEMOSTATIC INJECTABLE AGENTS
(including but not limited to: PlateMax IV, ThromboCare Injection,
PlateletBoost IV, and equivalent proprietary formulations):

Maximum Frequency         : 2 (two) administrations per 24-hour period
Minimum Gap Between Doses : 6 (six) hours between administrations
Maximum Duration          : As prescribed, not to exceed 5 consecutive days
Standard Dose Per Admin   : 1 vial (as per manufacturer specification)

BILLING COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENT:
Any hospital billing for platelet-supportive injectable agents must
match the frequency documented in the patient's prescription and nursing
administration record. Billing for more than 2 administrations of any
platelet-supportive injectable within any single 24-hour period will be
treated as:
(a) A billing discrepancy requiring mandatory clarification, AND
(b) A potential Fraud indicator under Section 9 of this Policy

The Insurance Company reserves the right to reject the portion of the
claim attributable to any administration beyond the clinically approved
frequency and to refer the matter to its Fraud Investigation Unit.

5.3 ANTIBIOTIC AND ANTIVIRAL PROTOCOL
Antibiotics and antiviral agents must be billed only for the duration
prescribed. Billing beyond the prescription period without a documented
extension note from the treating physician will not be covered.

5.4 CONSUMABLES AND DISPOSABLES
Consumable charges must be itemised individually in the hospital bill.
Lump-sum consumable charges exceeding INR 500 per day will require
supporting documentation from the hospital.

SECTION 6 — CASHLESS CLAIM PROCEDURE
================================================================================

6.1 PRE-AUTHORISATION REQUEST
For planned hospitalisation, the Insured Person or Hospital must submit
a Pre-Authorisation Request Form to the TPA/Insurance Company at least
72 hours prior to admission.

For emergency hospitalisation, Pre-Auth must be submitted within
24 hours of admission.

6.2 DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR PRE-AUTH
(a) Duly completed Pre-Authorisation Request Form (hospital letterhead)
(b) Copy of Insurance Policy / E-card
(c) Government photo ID of Insured Person
(d) Treating doctor's certificate with diagnosis and proposed treatment
(e) Relevant diagnostic reports if available

6.3 PRE-AUTH EVALUATION PROCESS
Upon receipt of the Pre-Auth Form, the Insurance Company shall:
Step 1: Verify policy status and active coverage
Step 2: Check applicable waiting periods under Section 3
Step 3: Validate the diagnosis against covered conditions
Step 4: Approve, query, or reject within 2 hours (emergency) or
        6 hours (planned) of receiving complete documents

6.4 AUTOMATIC PRE-AUTH REJECTION — WAITING PERIOD
If the Pre-Auth evaluation under Step 2 confirms that the proposed
admission falls within an applicable waiting period, the Pre-Auth shall
be automatically rejected without proceeding to clinical review.

A rejection letter citing the specific waiting period clause shall be
issued to the hospital and the Insured Person within 2 hours.

No further review is required for waiting-period-based rejections.

SECTION 8 — EXCLUSIONS
================================================================================

8.1 PERMANENT EXCLUSIONS (NOT COVERED UNDER ANY CIRCUMSTANCES)
(a) Cosmetic or aesthetic treatment
(b) Self-inflicted injuries
(c) Treatment arising from war, nuclear, or radioactive events
(d) Experimental treatments not approved by ICMR/WHO
(e) Treatment for alcohol or substance abuse

8.2 WAITING PERIOD EXCLUSIONS
Any illness or condition for which the applicable waiting period under
Section 3 has not been completed at the time of admission.

Specifically: Any claim where the date of admission is within 24 months
of the original Policy Start Date for general hospitalisation shall be
rejected under Clause 3.2 regardless of the nature of illness.

8.3 EXCESS BILLING EXCLUSION
Any portion of a hospital bill that exceeds the clinically approved
dosage or frequency of treatment as defined under Section 5 shall be
excluded from coverage.

The Insurance Company shall pay the portion of the claim that is
clinically compliant and reject the excess portion with documented reasons.

8.4 FRAUD-RELATED EXCLUSION
If an investigation under Section 9 confirms fraudulent billing, the
entire claim associated with the fraudulent transaction may be rejected
and the policy may be cancelled without refund of premium.

SECTION 9 — FRAUD DETECTION AND INVESTIGATION
================================================================================

9.1 FRAUD INDICATORS
The following shall be treated as automatic fraud indicators requiring
mandatory investigation:

(a) BILLING FREQUENCY ANOMALY
    Any injectable or medication billed at a frequency exceeding the
    clinically approved protocol defined in Section 5.

(b) DUPLICATE PROCEDURE BILLING
    Same procedure billed more than once on the same date without
    documented medical justification.

(c) PRESCRIPTION-BILLING MISMATCH
    Medications or injectables billed that are not listed in the
    treating doctor's prescription.

(d) COST ANOMALY
    Any line item billed at more than 200% of the prevailing market
    rate or CGHS/ESI rate for that item or procedure.

(e) IMPOSSIBLE CLINICAL COMBINATION
    Procedures or diagnoses billed together that are clinically
    contradictory or impossible (e.g., same patient undergoing
    contradictory surgical procedures on the same date).

9.2 INVESTIGATION PROCESS
Upon detection of one or more fraud indicators:
Step 1: Claim flagged and moved to Fraud Review Queue
Step 2: Hospital or provider notified within 24 hours
Step 3: Hospital must submit original nursing records, doctor's notes,
        and pharmacy dispensing records within 5 working days
Step 4: Insurance Company Fraud Unit reviews submitted evidence
Step 5: Decision issued within 15 working days

9.3 PARTIAL CLAIM SETTLEMENT — FRAUD DETECTED
If fraud is confirmed in one line item of the bill but the rest of the
claim is valid, the Insurance Company may:
(a) Approve the valid portion of the claim
(b) Reject the fraudulent line item with documented reasoning
(c) Blacklist the hospital from the network if fraud is repeated

9.4 FULL CLAIM REJECTION — FRAUD CONFIRMED
If the fraudulent billing materially alters the nature of the entire
claim, the Insurance Company reserves the right to reject the full claim
and refer the matter to IRDAI and relevant authorities.
